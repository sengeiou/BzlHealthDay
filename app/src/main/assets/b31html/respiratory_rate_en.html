Medical examination

During sleep, the normal breathing rate is 18-26 times per minute. The average and the highest value exceeding 18-26 can be judged as high the respiratory rate. If it exceeds a certain range, breathing difficulties may occur. If the condition is serious, must be sent to hospital for rescue in time.

Glossary

The medical term for the number of breaths per minute. One up and down of the chest is a breath, that is, one breath in and one out.

Typical symptoms

①Breathing too slow: The rhythm of the breathing is neat but the frequency is less than 10 beats/min, which is common in the end of life or before the apnea. Menstruation, metabolic diseases, or excessive intake of certain medicine can also cause hypothermia due to inhibition of the respiratory center.

②Breathing too fast: shortness of breath, feeling respiratory distress.

Treatment

For Difficulty breathing, the emergency actions at home are as follows:

①Keep quiet and avoid emotional stress to prevent worse breathing difficulties.

②Take a semi-recumbent position or a sitting position to reduce fatigue and oxygen consumption.

③Keep indoor air fresh and ventilated.

④Give a light diet and encourage patients to eat more vegetables and fruits to replenish the body's water.

⑤Appropriate phlegm and antispasmodic medicine such as Bibiping, aminophylline, etc. to keep the respiratory tract smooth.

⑥When the condition is serious, send patients to a nearby hospital for rescue.
