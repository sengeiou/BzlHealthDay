Common sense of Heart load

The cardiac load is divided into:

①cardiac preload;

② cardiac post-load.

Cardiac preload, also known as volumetric load, refers to the load before myocardial contraction, which is actually a response to ventricular end-diastolic volume or end-diastolic wall tension. Post-load refers to the resistance or load after myocardial contraction, also known as pressure load. Aortic pressure and pulmonary artery pressure are the post-load of the left and right ventricles.

1、Cardiac preload

The major factors of increased cardiac preload and possible heart problems:

①Valvular lesions, the second, tricuspid and aortic regurgitation can increase the volume load, and the narrowing of the mitral and tricuspid valves can reduce the volume load.

②Internal and external shunt diseases: atrial septal, ventricular septal defect and patent ductus arteriosus can increase the load.

③Systemic blood volume changes: a large amount of fluid, hyperthyroidism, chronic anemia, etc. in a short period of time can increase the load.Hyperkine, diarrhea, blood loss, etc. lead to a reduction in effective circulating blood volume, which can reduce the preload. Left ventricle is in aorta, mitral regurgitation, and congenital heart disease from right to left or left to right; right ventricle is in atrial septal defect, pulmonary valve or tricuspid regurgitation; two ventricles pressure Excessive load: severe anemia, hyperthyroidism, beriberi heart disease and arteriovenous fistula.

2、Cardiac Post-load

The major factors of increased cardiac post-load and possible heart problems:

① Aortic compliance: that is the ability of the aortic content to expand with the increase of the wall of the vessel, with thickening of the vessel wall, the compliance is reduced.

②Peripheral vascular resistance: It depends on the transverse area of the small arterial vascular bed and vascular tone, the latter is affected by vascular and humoral factors.

③Blood viscosity: When the blood viscosity is increased, the peripheral vascular resistance is increased.

④Circulating blood volume. Among them, peripheral vascular resistance is the most important, and it is often used as an indicator of left ventricular post-load in clinic. Left ventricular post-load is in hypertension, aortic outflow tract obstruction (aortic stenosis, aortic coarctation); right ventricular post-load is in pulmonary hypertension, pulmonary stenosis, obstructive pulmonary disease and pulmonary embolism.
